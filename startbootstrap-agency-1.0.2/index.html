How it Works

Discover your social network as the night sky

Friends as stars

Look at your friends as the stars in the night sky. The size of the star represents the number of books your friend has read. The closer your friend is to the center, the more common books you have with your friend. You can also discover matches between your friends.

Browse through your books

All your books are shown in the right scroll bar. They are ordered by popularity within your network: you will find the books that most of your friends have read at the top, while the books that only you have read at the bottom.

Discover constellations

When selecting a book by clicking it, all friends who have read that book will be connected by the lines of a constellation on your sky.

Background

Skylike. A universal approach to visualizing social media.

Skylike is a web application that uses data from Goodreads API and visualizes it as a night sky full of stars. In the application you can browse the books of your account and detect connections between your friends and the books that they have read as constellations.

Social media is an integral part of our everyday lives. As the amount of content on social networks grows rapidly over time, there is very little possibility for the individual to explore this information in a meaningful way. In order to facilitate such a task, some social networks provide built-in information visualization tools that allow their users to get an overview of their own and their friend’s content, but these are few and far in-between.

The field of semiotics studies the metaphor as a powerful rhetoric function, which helps people understand the world around them by using familiar structures. One of the most familiar sights for the human eye is the night sky and its stars. Most of us are able to recognize Orion and he's belt on the winter night sky, or notice a bright triangle on the summer sky. We have always had the tendencies to give meaning to the shapes that formed in our imagination by connecting the stars of the sky.

Skylike is based on the idea of visualizing social networks content as the familiar structure of the night sky. We have put our user into the center of the universe, much like how ancient explorers saw Earth as the center of the world. The stars in the sky represent our user's connections/friends in the context of the social network. The stars are connected by common likes or interests.

In this instance of Skylike, we have chosen Goodreads to be our universe. Goodreads is a social network that helps its users keep track of the books they have read or that they want to read and keep in touch with their friends' interest and reads. Our users are Goodreads' books enthusiasts, and our goal is to help them find connections between themselves and their friends: to explore the common books they have read, and the dynamics of their networks.

We aim at providing a magical experience to our universe explorers. We help them keep an overview of their friends in the form of stars in the sky. They can explore their books and find out which ones are more popular in their networks, as constellations appear to highlight their friends who have read the same books.
